Giraffe

In the wild these beautiful creatures live for around 25 years. In captivity they live longer, and have been known to reach 40 years old.

  • DIET: all trees, typically acacia trees
  • RANGE: Giraffes can be found in central, eastern and southern
  • BEHAVIOR: Giraffes are non-territorial, social animals
  • THREATS: Giraffes are hunted for their meat, coat and tails.

Characterized by its long legs, long neck, and distinctive spotted pattern, many people first believed the giraffe was a cross between a leopard and a camel, which is reflected in its scientific name, Giraffa camelopardalis. Fully grown giraffes stand 4.3–5.7 m (14.1–18.7 ft) tall, with males taller than females.
